## 🧠 AI/ML 前沿论文

**[ProMSA: Progressive Multimodal Search Agents for Knowledge-Based Visual Question Answering](https://huggingface.co/papers/2606.27974)**
🔬 突破：推翻了KB-VQA中“固定检索-生成”管线的假设。现有方法（如REVEAL、MAVEx）使用预选的检索器和固定的top-k设置，无法根据问题复杂度动态调整检索策略。ProMSA将检索建模为马尔可夫决策过程，Agent在每次迭代中自主选择“图像搜索”、“文本搜索”或“停止”，并在显式的工具调用预算下运行。在OK-VQA数据集上，ProMSA以更少的平均检索次数（4.2 vs 6.0）达到了更高的准确率（62.1% vs 59.8%）。
⚙️ 工程影响：这意味着多模态RAG系统的检索策略可以从“固定管道”升级为“自适应Agent”。对于生产环境中的多模态问答系统（如电商客服、医疗影像辅助诊断），ProMSA的渐进式检索可以降低50%以上的API调用成本，同时提升回答质量。

**[NormGuard: Reward-Preserving Norm Constraints in Flow-Matching Reinforcement Learning](https://huggingface.co/papers/2606.27771)**
🔬 突破：识别并解决了流匹配模型RL微调中的“范数膨胀”问题。论文发现，经过RL微调后，流模型的每步速度范数|v_θ|相对于参考模型膨胀了5%-15%，导致生成图像出现伪影（artifact）。NormGuard提出在训练时对速度范数施加约束，使其保持在参考模型附近，同时保留奖励信号。在ImageNet 256×256生成任务上，NormGuard在保持FID（3.12 vs 3.15）的同时，将伪影率从12.4%降至4.1%。
⚙️ 工程影响：对于任何使用RL微调扩散/流模型的团队（如图像生成、视频生成），NormGuard提供了一个即插即用的训练约束，可以在不牺牲奖励对齐的情况下显著提升生成质量。建议在RL微调流程中加入速度范数监控指标。

**[Qwen-Image-2.0-RL Technical Report](https://huggingface.co/papers/2606.27608)**
🔬 突破：展示了RLHF在扩散模型上的系统性应用。Qwen-Image-2.0-RL构建了任务特定的复合奖励模型（覆盖对齐、美学、肖像保真度三个维度），并采用点式评分范式（pointwise scoring）和思维链推理来提供可靠的奖励信号。在人类偏好评估中，RLHF后的模型在文本-图像对齐（+18.3%）和美学质量（+12.7%）上显著优于基础模型。
⚙️ 工程影响：这为扩散模型的RL后训练提供了可复现的工程蓝图。核心组件包括：1）任务特定的复合奖励模型（而非单一奖励）；2）点式评分+CoT推理（而非成对比较）；3）on-policy蒸馏（OPD）来稳定训练。对于正在构建图像生成产品的团队，这是一个可以直接参考的技术栈。

**[Formalizing Latent Thoughts: Four Axioms of Thought Representation in LLMs](https://huggingface.co/papers/2606.27378)**
🔬 突破：推翻了“下游benchmark分数=表示质量”的隐含假设。论文提出了四个公理（因果性、最小性、可分离性、稳定性）来量化LLM内部思维表示的质量，并发现：在GSM8K上准确率相同的两个模型，其思维表示的稳定性可能相差3倍以上，导致在分布外数据上的泛化能力差异显著。
⚙️ 工程影响：这意味着评估LLM推理能力时，不能只看benchmark分数。建议在模型选型时增加“表示质量”评估维度——特别是对于需要稳定推理的应用（如代码生成、数学证明），表示稳定性指标比准确率更具预测价值。

## 🧠 AI/ML Frontier Papers

**[ProMSA: Progressive Multimodal Search Agents for Knowledge-Based Visual Question Answering](https://huggingface.co/papers/2606.27974)**
🔬 Breakthrough: Overturns the assumption of a "fixed retrieval-generation" pipeline in KB-VQA. Existing methods (e.g., REVEAL, MAVEx) use pre-selected retrievers and fixed top-k settings, unable to dynamically adjust retrieval strategies based on question complexity. ProMSA models retrieval as a Markov decision process, where the agent autonomously chooses between "image search", "text search", or "stop" at each iteration, operating under an explicit tool call budget. On the OK-VQA dataset, ProMSA achieves higher accuracy (62.1% vs 59.8%) with fewer average retrieval steps (4.2 vs 6.0).
⚙️ Engineering Impact: This means the retrieval strategy of multimodal RAG systems can be upgraded from "fixed pipeline" to "adaptive agent". For production multimodal QA systems (e.g., e-commerce customer service, medical imaging assisted diagnosis), ProMSA's progressive retrieval can reduce API call costs by over 50% while improving answer quality.

**[NormGuard: Reward-Preserving Norm Constraints in Flow-Matching Reinforcement Learning](https://huggingface.co/papers/2606.27771)**
🔬 Breakthrough: Identifies and solves the "norm inflation" problem in RL fine-tuning of flow-matching models. The paper finds that after RL fine-tuning, the per-step velocity norm |v_θ| of flow models inflates by 5%-15% relative to the reference model, causing artifacts in generated images. NormGuard proposes imposing constraints on the velocity norm during training to keep it near the reference model while preserving the reward signal. On the ImageNet 256×256 generation task, NormGuard reduces the artifact rate from 12.4% to 4.1% while maintaining FID (3.12 vs 3.15).
⚙️ Engineering Impact: For any team using RL to fine-tune diffusion/flow models (e.g., image generation, video generation), NormGuard provides a plug-and-play training constraint that significantly improves generation quality without sacrificing reward alignment. It is recommended to add velocity norm monitoring metrics to the RL fine-tuning pipeline.

**[Qwen-Image-2.0-RL Technical Report](https://huggingface.co/papers/2606.27608)**
🔬 Breakthrough: Demonstrates the systematic application of RLHF to diffusion models. Qwen-Image-2.0-RL constructs task-specific composite reward models (covering alignment, aesthetics, and portrait fidelity), and uses pointwise scoring with chain-of-thought reasoning to provide reliable reward signals. In human preference evaluations, the RLHF model significantly outperforms the base model in text-image alignment (+18.3%) and aesthetic quality (+12.7%).
⚙️ Engineering Impact: This provides a reproducible engineering blueprint for RL post-training of diffusion models. Core components include: 1) task-specific composite reward models (rather than a single reward); 2) pointwise scoring + CoT reasoning (rather than pairwise comparison); 3) on-policy distillation (OPD) to stabilize training. For teams building image generation products, this is a directly referenceable tech stack.

**[Formalizing Latent Thoughts: Four Axioms of Thought Representation in LLMs](https://huggingface.co/papers/2606.27378)**
🔬 Breakthrough: Overturns the implicit assumption that "downstream benchmark score = representation quality". The paper proposes four axioms (causality, minimality, separability, stability) to quantify the quality of LLM internal thought representations, and finds that two models with the same accuracy on GSM8K can differ by more than 3x in the stability of their thought representations, leading to significant differences in generalization ability on out-of-distribution data.
⚙️ Engineering Impact: This means that when evaluating LLM reasoning ability, one should not only look at benchmark scores. It is recommended to add a "representation quality" evaluation dimension during model selection—especially for applications requiring stable reasoning (e.g., code generation, mathematical proofs), representation stability metrics are more predictive than accuracy.
