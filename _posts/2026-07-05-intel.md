**[Leaking YouTube creators' private videos](https://javoriuski.com/post/youtube)** 👍498 💬280
🗣 Community debate: YouTube's "private video" permission model has a fundamental flaw—the API can enumerate all video IDs in a channel (including private ones), and access is possible as long as the video ID is known. 🗣 Community debate: Claude Code's workspace isolation mechanism has a serious vulnerability—workspace instances of different users may share session/cache, leading to data leakage. The core engineering conclusion is: token compression (encoding multiple reasoning steps into one token) reduces inference costs but sacrifices the "decomposability" of reasoning—the model cannot correct errors at intermediate steps. This is a classic trade-off between "efficiency vs. quality" in AI model optimization.
