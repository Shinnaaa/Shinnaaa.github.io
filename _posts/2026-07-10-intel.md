## 🧠 AI/ML 前沿论文

**[Flash-BoN: Instant Drafts for Inference-Time Scaling in Diffusion Models](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.04461)**
🔬 突破：推翻了“扩散模型推理时扩展（Inference-time Scaling）必须通过增加去噪步数来实现”的假设。Flash-BoN提出“即时草稿”机制：在单次去噪过程中并行生成多个候选轨迹，通过轻量级验证器（<1ms）在中间步骤进行筛选，将Best-of-N的推理延迟降低至原来的1/N（N=候选数）。在标准壁钟时间评估下，Flash-BoN比传统BoN（需N次完整去噪）快3-5倍，同时保持相同的FID分数。
⚙️ 工程影响：这意味着**扩散模型的推理时扩展不再需要增加硬件成本**——你可以在单张A100上实现原本需要N张卡才能达到的生成质量。对部署团队而言，可以在不增加GPU预算的情况下，将图像生成质量提升一个档次。

**[Jet-Long: Efficient Long-Context Extension with Dynamic Bifocal RoPE](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.07740)**
🔬 突破：改进了现有零样本长上下文扩展方法（如YaRN、NTK-aware）的“单一缩放因子”假设。Jet-Long提出“动态双焦RoPE”：对短距离token使用保守缩放（保持短上下文精度），对长距离token使用激进缩放（支持超长上下文），且缩放因子根据输入长度动态调整。在128K上下文测试中，Jet-Long在RULER基准上比YaRN提升12%，同时短上下文（4K）的准确率下降<0.5%（YaRN下降3.2%）。
⚙️ 工程影响：这意味着**部署长上下文LLM不再需要在“短上下文精度”和“长上下文能力”之间做取舍**。对于RAG和Agentic Workflow场景，可以直接将上下文窗口从32K扩展到128K，而无需微调或重新训练。

**[TESSERA v2: Scaling Pixel-wise Earth Foundation Models](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.03949)**
🔬 突破：推翻了“预训练损失越低，下游任务性能越好”的普遍假设。TESSERA v2在395次训练实验中发现：预训练损失与15个下游任务的性能相关性极低（|Pearson r| < 0.2），这意味着**用损失选择模型会浪费大量算力**。他们提出“下游任务感知的模型选择策略”：在预训练过程中定期对少量下游任务进行快速评估，选择评估分数最高的checkpoint而非损失最低的。在相同算力预算下，该策略将下游任务平均性能提升8.3%。
⚙️ 工程影响：这意味着**基础模型的预训练不再是一个“黑盒”过程**——团队应该建立“预训练-下游评估”的闭环，而不是盲目追求更低的训练损失。对于自建基础模型的团队，建议在预训练管线中加入每10K步的快速下游评估。

## 🧠 AI/ML Frontier Papers

**[Flash-BoN: Instant Drafts for Inference-Time Scaling in Diffusion Models](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.04461)**
🔬 Breakthrough: Overturns the assumption that "inference-time scaling for diffusion models must be achieved by increasing the number of denoising steps." Flash-BoN proposes an "instant draft" mechanism: generate multiple candidate trajectories in parallel during a single denoising process, use a lightweight verifier (<1ms) to filter at intermediate steps, reducing the inference latency of Best-of-N to 1/N of the original (N = number of candidates). Under standard wall-clock time evaluation, Flash-BoN is 3-5x faster than traditional BoN (requiring N complete denoising runs) while maintaining the same FID score.
⚙️ Engineering Impact: This means **inference-time scaling for diffusion models no longer requires increased hardware costs**—you can achieve generation quality on a single A100 that previously required N cards. For deployment teams, this allows improving image generation quality by one level without increasing the GPU budget.

**[Jet-Long: Efficient Long-Context Extension with Dynamic Bifocal RoPE](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.07740)**
🔬 Breakthrough: Improves the "single scaling factor" assumption of existing zero-shot long-context extension methods (e.g., YaRN, NTK-aware). Jet-Long proposes "Dynamic Bifocal RoPE": use conservative scaling for short-distance tokens (maintaining short-context accuracy) and aggressive scaling for long-distance tokens (supporting ultra-long contexts), with the scaling factor dynamically adjusted based on input length. In 128K context tests, Jet-Long improves by 12% over YaRN on the RULER benchmark, while the accuracy drop on short contexts (4K) is <0.5% (YaRN drops 3.2%).
⚙️ Engineering Impact: This means **deploying long-context LLMs no longer requires a trade-off between "short-context accuracy" and "long-context capability."** For RAG and Agentic Workflow scenarios, the context window can be directly extended from 32K to 128K without fine-tuning or retraining.

**[TESSERA v2: Scaling Pixel-wise Earth Foundation Models](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.03949)**
🔬 Breakthrough: Overturns the common assumption that "lower pre-training loss leads to better downstream task performance." In 395 training experiments, TESSERA v2 found that the correlation between pre-training loss and performance on 15 downstream tasks is extremely low (|Pearson r| < 0.2), meaning **selecting models based on loss wastes significant compute**. They propose a "downstream task-aware model selection strategy": periodically perform quick evaluations on a small set of downstream tasks during pre-training, selecting the checkpoint with the highest evaluation score rather than the lowest loss. Under the same compute budget, this strategy improves average downstream task performance by 8.3%.
⚙️ Engineering Impact: This means **pre-training of foundation models is no longer a "black box" process**—teams should establish a "pre-training → downstream evaluation" loop instead of blindly pursuing lower training loss. For teams building their own foundation models, it is recommended to add quick downstream evaluations every 10K steps in the pre-training pipeline.
